Abstract

We present PLUME, a latent reasoning framework for training universal multi-modal embedding models. PLUME introduces a Latent Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) transition layer that enables vision-language models to perform implicit reasoning in a continuous latent space, rather than relying on explicit chain-of-thought token generation. This approach allows the model to develop richer representations for complex multi-modal understanding tasks including image retrieval, video understanding, and visual document comprehension. Built upon Qwen2-VL-2B, PLUME achieves strong performance on the MMEB benchmark across image, video, and visual document modalities, demonstrating the effectiveness of latent reasoning for multi-modal embedding.

Method Overview

PLUME extends standard vision-language models with a Latent MoE Transition mechanism. Instead of generating explicit reasoning tokens, the model processes latent thought tokens through a mixture-of-experts layer consisting of 4 routed experts and 1 shared expert. A curriculum learning strategy progressively increases the number of latent reasoning steps during training, enabling the model to develop increasingly sophisticated reasoning capabilities.
